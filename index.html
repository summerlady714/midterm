About Me

I'm Crys your driver for the evening. I have car chargers for both Apple and Android, cough drops, gum, hand sanitizer, tissue, barf bags, and wifi for the kiddo's tablets. This is the spiel riders hear as they settle in for the ride.

I'm going to answer the typical questions that i get as a driver. This is my secondary income.I only drive at night Thursday thru Saturday and during the day Sunday. No I'm not afraid to drive at night as I am pretty well equipped for self-defense. I don't mind long rides because I love to drive, so of course I'll take you into the city. One of my longest rides was from Lebanon New Jersey to Greenwich CT. I call myself NJ Road Queen because I have seen much of New Jersey from tip to tip.

Do's and Don'ts As A Rideshare Driver

  • Keep a clean vehicle.
  • Have amenities!
  • Be open to giving the passenger the aux.
  • Have EZ pass.
  • Make sure the temperature is comfortable for you and your passengers.
  • Don't talk politics!
  • Learn to use the GPS without sound.
  • Be aware of strange invites after the ride.
  • Know where bathrooms are in your typical areas.
  • Keep a cool head.

Do's and Don'ts As A Rider

  • Put on your seatbelt.
  • Always ask if it's ok to sit up front.
  • Be polite.
  • Don't talk politics.
  • Call to see how far your driver is willing to go.
  • Don't ask to fill the car beyond capacity
  • If you choose the pool option, expect other riders.
  • Don't hit on the driver.
  • Tip your driver.
